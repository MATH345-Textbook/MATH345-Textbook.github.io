Definition 1.5.1. Summing Column Vectors and Scalar Multiples of Row and Column Vectors.

A column vector is a matrix consisting of a single column, and a row vector is a matrix consisting of a single row.
Let \(\mathbf{x}\) and \(\mathbf{y}\) be column vectors of the same size, and let \(k\) be a scalar/real number. We define their sum \(\mathbf{x}+\mathbf{y}\) by adding their respective entries and the scalar product of \(k\) with \(\mathbf{x}\text{,}\) denoted \(k\mathbf{x}\text{,}\) by multiplying each entry of \(\mathbf{x}\) by \(k\text{.}\)
In other words, if
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}=\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ \vdots \\x_n\end{bmatrix} \quad\text{and}\quad \mathbf{y}=\begin{bmatrix} y_1 \\ y_2 \\ \vdots \\y_n\end{bmatrix}\text{,} \end{equation*}
then
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}+ \mathbf{y}=\begin{bmatrix} x_1+y_1 \\ x_2+y_2 \\ \vdots \\x_n+y_n\end{bmatrix} \quad\text{and}\quad k\mathbf{x}=\begin{bmatrix} kx_1 \\ kx_2 \\ \vdots \\kx_n\end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Compare these definitions to those in Definition 1.1.7 and Definition 1.1.10.
A column vector obtained from the sum of scalar multiples of several column vectors is called a linear combination of those column vectors. For example, if \(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y}, \mathbf{z}\) are column vectors, then for any choice of scalars \(r,s,t\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} r\mathbf{x}+s\mathbf{y}+t\mathbf{z} \end{equation*}
is a linear combination of \(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y}\) and \(\mathbf{z}\text{.}\)
Sums, scalar products, and linear combinations of row vectors are defined analogously.
