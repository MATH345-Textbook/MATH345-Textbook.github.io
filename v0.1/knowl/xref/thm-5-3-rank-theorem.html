Theorem 4.3.6. The Rank Theorem.

Let \(A\) denote any \(m \times n\) matrix of rank \(r\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} \dimens(\col(A)) = \dimens(\row(A)) = r\text{.} \end{equation*}
Moreover, if \(A\) is row-equivalent to a matrix \(R\) in row echelon form, then
  • The \(r\) leading rows of \(R\) are a basis for \(\row(A)\text{.}\)
  • If the pivots of \(R\) lie in columns \(j_1, j_2, \dots, j_r\text{,}\) then the columns \(j_1,j_2,\dots,j_r\) of \(A\) are a basis for \(\col(A)\text{.}\)
