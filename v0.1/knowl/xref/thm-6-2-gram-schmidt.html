Theorem 5.2.2. The Gram-Schmidt Orthogonalization Algorithm.

Let \(U\) be a subspace of \(\R^n\text{.}\) Then \(U\) has an orthogonal basis.
Given a basis \(\{ \mathbf{x}_1, \dots, \mathbf{x}_m \}\) of \(U\text{,}\) we can iteratively define an orthogonal basis \(\{ \mathbf{f}_1, \dots, \mathbf{f}_m \}\) of \(U\) by defining
\begin{align*} \mathbf{f}_1 \amp = \mathbf{x}_1\\ \mathbf{f}_2 \amp = \mathbf{x}_2 - \frac{\mathbf{x}_2 \cdot \mathbf{f}_1}{\|\mathbf{f}_1\|^2}\mathbf{f}_1\\ \mathbf{f}_3 \amp = \mathbf{x}_3 - \frac{\mathbf{x}_3 \cdot \mathbf{f}_1}{\|\mathbf{f}_1\|^2}\mathbf{f}_1 - \frac{\mathbf{x}_3 \cdot \mathbf{f}_2}{\|\mathbf{f}_2\|^2}\mathbf{f}_2 \end{align*}
and so on, i.e. for \(2 \leq k \leq m\) defining
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{f}_i = \mathbf{x}_k - \frac{\mathbf{x}_k \cdot \mathbf{f}_1}{\|\mathbf{f}_1\|^2}\mathbf{f}_1 - \frac{\mathbf{x}_k \cdot \mathbf{f}_2}{\|\mathbf{f}_2\|^2}\mathbf{f}_2 - \cdots - \frac{\mathbf{x}_k \cdot \mathbf{f}_{k-1}}{\|\mathbf{f}_{k-1}\|^2}\mathbf{f}_{k-1}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Then
  • \(\{\mathbf{f}_1, \mathbf{f}_2, \ldots, \mathbf{f}_m\}\) is an orthogonal basis of \(U\text{.}\)
  • \(\spans \{\mathbf{f}_1, \mathbf{f}_2, \ldots, \mathbf{f}_k\} = \spans \{\mathbf{x}_1, \mathbf{x}_2, \ldots, \mathbf{x}_k\}\) for each \(k = 1, 2, \ldots, m\text{.}\)
