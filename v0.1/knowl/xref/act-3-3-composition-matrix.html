Activity 2.3.2. Matrix Transformations and Composition.

Let \(A\) be \(m\times n\) and \(B\) be \(n\times k\text{,}\) and build the induced matrix transformations \(T_A:\R^n\to \R^m\text{,}\) and \(T_B:\R^k\to\R^n\text{.}\) Consider the composition of \(T_A\) and \(T_B\text{,}\) i.e. the transformation \((T_A \circ T_B): \R^k \to \R^m\) defined by \((T_A\circ T_B)\mathbf{x}=T_A(T_B(\mathbf{x}))\text{.}\) Compute \((T_A\circ T_B)\mathbf{x}\) for a general \(\mathbf{X} \in \R^k\text{.}\)
Solution.
Let
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}=\begin{bmatrix}x_1\\\vdots\\x_k\end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
By the definition of the matrix-vector product (Recall Definition 2.2.2):
\begin{equation*} B\mathbf{x} = x_1\mathbf{b}_1 + \cdots + x_k\mathbf{b}_k\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(\mathbf{b}_j\) is column \(j\) of \(B\text{.}\)
Therefore:
\begin{align*} (\amp T_A \circ T_B)\mathbf{x}\\ \amp = T_A(T_B(\mathbf{x}))\\ \amp = A(B\mathbf{x})\\ \amp = A(x_1\mathbf{b}_1 + \cdots + x_k\mathbf{b}_k)\\ \amp = x_1 (A\mathbf{b}_1) + \cdots + x_k (A\mathbf{b}_k) \amp\text{(by distributivity, as in} \knowl{./knowl/xref/thm-2-2-distributive-properties.html}{\text{Theorem 2.2.3}}\text{)}\\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix}A\mathbf{b}_1 & \cdots & A\mathbf{b}_k\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}x_1\\\vdots\\x_k\end{bmatrix}\\ \amp = (AB)\mathbf{x} \amp\text{(by definition of the matrix product)}\\ \amp = T_{AB}\mathbf{x} \end{align*}
