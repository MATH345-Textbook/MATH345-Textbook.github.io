Activity 1.4.4. Solving a Linear System Using Row Echelon Form.

Solve the linear system
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ccccccccc} x \amp + \amp 2y \amp + \amp 3z \amp = \amp 9\\ 2x\amp - \amp y \amp + \amp z \amp = \amp 8\\ 3x\amp \amp \amp - \amp z \amp = \amp 3 \end{array} \end{equation*}
by using Algorithm 1.4.6 to find a matrix in row echelon form which is row equivalent to the augmented matrix \([A \mid \mathbf{b}]\text{.}\)
Solution.
Augmented matrix:
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \amp 9 \\ 2 \amp -1 \amp 1 \amp 8 \\ 3 \amp 0 \amp -1 \amp 3 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
Subtract 2 times Row 1 from Row 2 and subtract 3 times Row 1 from Row 3:
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \amp 9 \\ 0 \amp -5 \amp -5 \amp -10 \\ 0 \amp -6 \amp -10 \amp -24 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
Multiply Row 2 by \(-1/5\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \amp 9 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp 1 \amp 2 \\ 0 \amp -6 \amp -10 \amp -24 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
Add 6 times Row 2 to Row 3:
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \amp 9 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp 1 \amp 2 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp -4 \amp -12 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
Multiply Row 3 by \(-1/4\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \amp 9 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp 1 \amp 2 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 1 \amp 3 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
This matrix is the augmented of the system of linear equations
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ccccccccc} x \amp + \amp 2y \amp + \amp 3z \amp = \amp 9\\ \amp \amp y \amp + \amp z \amp = \amp 2\\ \amp \amp \amp \amp z \amp = \amp 3 \end{array} \end{equation*}
By Theorem 1.4.7, we know that this system is equivalent to the original system of equations specified by the problem. We now apply back substitution to solve the system:
Solving for \(z\)
Row 3 tells us \(z = 3\text{.}\)
Solving for \(y\)
Row 2 gives \(y + z = 2\text{,}\) and since \(z = 3\text{,}\) we calculate that \(y = -1\text{.}\)
Solving for \(x\)
Row 1 gives \(x + 2y + 3z = 9\text{,}\) and since \(y = -1\) and \(z = 3\text{,}\) we calculate that \(x = 4\text{.}\)
So the unique solution is given by \(x=4\text{,}\) \(y=-1\text{,}\) \(z=3\)
