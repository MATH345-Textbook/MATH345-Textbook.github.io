Definition 1.3.7. Matrices Corresponding To Linear Equations.

The coefficient matrix of a linear system of the form (1.3.1) is the \(m\times n\) matrix
\begin{equation*} A = \begin{bmatrix} a_{11} \amp a_{12} \amp \cdots \amp a_{1n} \\ a_{21} \amp a_{22} \amp \cdots \amp a_{2n} \\ \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \ddots \amp \vdots \\ a_{m1} \amp a_{m2} \amp \cdots \amp a_{mn} \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
The constant matrix, also called the constant vector or constant column vector is
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{b}=\begin{bmatrix} b_1 \\ b_2 \\ \vdots \\ b_m\end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
We can adjoin \(\mathbf{b}\) to matrix \(A\) to create the augmented matrix representing our linear system:
\begin{align*} [A \mid \mathbf{b}] \amp = \begin{bmatrix} a_{11} \amp a_{12} \amp \cdots \amp a_{1n} \amp b_1 \\ a_{21} \amp a_{22} \amp \cdots \amp a_{2n} \amp b_2 \\ \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \ddots \amp \vdots \amp \vdots \\ a_{m1} \amp a_{m2} \amp \cdots \amp a_{mn} \amp b_m \end{bmatrix} \end{align*}
