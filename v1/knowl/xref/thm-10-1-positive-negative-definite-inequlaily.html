Theorem 9.5.8.

A symmetric matrix \(A\) is positive definite if and only if \(\mathbf{x}^T A \mathbf{x} > 0\) for every vector \(\mathbf{x} \neq \mathbf{0}\) in \(\R^n\text{.}\) It is negative definite if and only if \(\mathbf{x}^T A \mathbf{x} \lt 0\) for every vector \(\mathbf{x} \neq \mathbf{0}\text{.}\)

Proof.

This follows from Theorem 9.5.5.
