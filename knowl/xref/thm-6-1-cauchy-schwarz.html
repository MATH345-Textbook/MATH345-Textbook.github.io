Theorem 4.1.2. The Cauchy-Schwarz inequality.

If \(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y}\) are vectors in \(\R^n\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} | \mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{y} | \leq \| \mathbf{x} \| \| \mathbf{y} \|\text{.} \end{equation*}
Moreover, \(| \mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{y} | = \| \mathbf{x} \| \| \mathbf{y} \|\) only when \(\mathbf{x}\) and \(\mathbf{y}\) are scalar multiples of each other.
