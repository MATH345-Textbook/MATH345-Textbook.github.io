Theorem 7.2.3.

Let \(E=\{\mathbf{e}_1, \mathbf{e}_2, \ldots, \mathbf{e}_n\}\) be the standard basis for \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{,}\) and let \(\langle \; , \; \rangle\) be any inner product on \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\) Let \(c_{ij}=\langle\mathbf{e}_i, \mathbf{e}_j\rangle\) and consider the matrix \(C=[c_{ij}]\text{.}\) Then
  1. \(C\) is a symmetric matrix.
    🔗
  2. \(\langle \mathbf{u},\mathbf{v}\rangle= \mathbf{u}^TC\mathbf{v}\) for all \(\mathbf{u},\mathbf{v}\) in \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\)
