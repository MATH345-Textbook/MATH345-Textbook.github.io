Theorem 3.3.4. Limit of a Vector-Valued Function.
Let \(\mathbf{r}(t): \R \to \R^n\) be a function with component functions \(r_1,\dots,r_n: \R \to \R\text{.}\) Then if
\begin{equation*} \lim_{t \to a} r_1(t) = v_1 \quad \cdots \quad \lim_{t \to a} r_n(t) = v_n\text{,} \end{equation*}
and we define the vector \(\mathbf{v} = (v_1,\dots,v_n)\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \lim_{t \to a} \mathbf{r}(t) = \mathbf{v}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Equivalently,
\begin{equation*} \lim_{t \to a} \mathbf{r}(t) = \begin{bmatrix} \lim_{t \to a} r_1(t)\\ \vdots\\ \lim_{t \to a} r_n(t) \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
