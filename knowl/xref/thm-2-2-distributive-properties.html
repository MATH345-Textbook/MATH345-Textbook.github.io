Theorem 1.3.2. Properties of matrix-vector products.

Let \(A\) and \(B\) be \(m\times n\) matrices, let \(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}\in\mathbb R^n\text{,}\) and let \(r\) be a scalar. Then
  • \(A(\mathbf{x}+\mathbf{y})=A\mathbf{x}+A\mathbf{y}\text{,}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(A(r\mathbf{x})=r(A\mathbf{x})=(rA)\mathbf{x}\text{,}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \((A+B)\mathbf{x}=A\mathbf{x}+B\mathbf{x}\text{.}\)
