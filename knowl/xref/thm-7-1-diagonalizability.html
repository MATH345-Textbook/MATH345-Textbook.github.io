Theorem 5.3.10.

An \(n \times n\) matrix \(A\) is diagonalizable if and only if \(A\) has \(n\) eigenvectors \(\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2, \ldots, \mathbf{v}_n\) such that the matrix \(P\) with columns \(\mathbf{v}_1,\dots,\mathbf{v}_n\) is invertible. In this case, \(P^{-1}AP=D\text{,}\) where \(D\) is a diagonal matrix such that the elements of \(D\) on the main diagonal are the eigenvalues of \(A\text{.}\)
