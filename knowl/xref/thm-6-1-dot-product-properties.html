Theorem 4.1.5.

Let \(\mathbf{x}\text{,}\) \(\mathbf{y}\text{,}\) and \(\mathbf{z}\) be vectors in \(\R^n\text{,}\) and let \(a\) be a scalar in \(\R\text{.}\) Then the following properties hold:
  • \(\mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y} \cdot \mathbf{x}\text{.}\)
    🔗
  • \(\mathbf{x} \cdot (\mathbf{y} + \mathbf{z}) = \mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{y} + \mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{z}\text{.}\)
    🔗
  • \((a \mathbf{x}) \cdot \mathbf{y} = a (\mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{y}) = \mathbf{x} \cdot (a \mathbf{y})\text{.}\)
    🔗
  • \(\| \mathbf{x} \|^2 = \mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{x}\text{.}\)
    🔗
  • \(\mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{x} \geq 0\text{,}\) and \(\mathbf{x} \cdot \mathbf{x} = 0\) if and only if \(\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}\text{.}\)
    🔗
  • \(\| a \mathbf{x} \| = |a| \| \mathbf{x} \|\text{.}\)
    🔗
